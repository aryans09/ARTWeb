Why Focus On CS?

It’s the only thing we think we know, man. We can’t solve the problems of the entire world without getting our own house in order.Solving real problems is hard, making powerpoint decks and talking in conference calls is easy. Solving any problem requires razorsharp focus, ruthless execution, dedication and consistency in delivering value over a substantial period of time. Hence, we focus on our core competency and also the only problem CSA can meaningfully target – state of CS in BITS.

Why recently graduated alumni?

We revere our college heroes, many of whom are our immediate seniors. Will we listen to someone who has been out of BITS for 10 years, and blabbers about some high fundoo cloud architecture in a language that sounds nothing less than magic spells to us?
Probably not.
Will we listen to our immediate seniors who understand the pains we are currently going through, and offer us their stories about how they managed to do well?
Probably yes.

Why bother about research?

Both academics and industry are in dire need of people who can deliver groundbreaking results. One needs to understand the theoretical aspects, and be able to generalize them to produce anything of significance. Could these abilities be developed by discussing MySQL or Google developer APIs? Possibly not. We need to develop the ability to dive in deep into the CS concepts.

What is this platform thingy?

BITS has produced and will continue producing amazing CS alumni. Many of these folk end up inventing something new, or build a technical business and attack a problem that others haven’t attacked before. We believe that involvement and ideas from such alumni is crucial to the betterment of CS at BITS. At this point, ART has seen 6 successful editions with * (insert attendance numbers here). The overall satisfaction level of the audience and the speakers is high. Since the mechanics have been worked out, ART can and should be used as a platform to launch or spread the word about other alumni initiatives focused on CS.
